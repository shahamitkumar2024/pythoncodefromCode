# Co‑operative Bank (UK) – TCS Business Development Deck

## Slide 1: Executive Summary

The Co‑operative Bank closed FY 2024 with net interest income of **£411.4m** (down **12.3%** year‑on‑year from **£469.2m**), statutory profit before tax of **£93.2m** (–**6.2%** vs 2023), and underlying profit before tax of **£132.6m** (–**9.9%**), while maintaining a strong capital and liquidity position with a **CET1 ratio of 23.9%**, **total capital ratio of 27.7%**, **UK leverage ratio of 4.5%**, and **LCR of 201.8%**. The total customer loan book increased to **£20.1bn**, with growth in prime residential (**£16.5bn**), Buy to Let (**£2.3bn**) and corporate lending (**£0.6bn**), and tangible net asset value rose from **£0.84bn to £0.89bn**, but margin compression (NIM down from **1.87% to 1.57%**) and a weaker **underlying cost:income ratio (62% → 67%)** highlight structural efficiency and income‑generation pressures. Overall financial health is **STABLE**: profitability and buffers are sound, but sustainable growth and cost transformation are urgent priorities.

**3 Specific TCS Opportunity Statements**
1. **IT Simplification & Core Banking Platform Transformation:** Support the bank’s ongoing core banking platform migration and IT simplification using **TCS BaNCS**, **TCS MasterCraft** and **ignio AIOps** to accelerate decommissioning of legacy systems, reduce the **67%** underlying cost:income ratio, and turn the **£33.6m** of 2024 strategic expenditure into measurable efficiency gains.
2. **Digital Channels & Customer Experience Modernisation:** Re‑platform and enhance mobile/online banking journeys, integrating with the new core and analytics (e.g., **TCS AI.Cloud** and TCS customer intelligence solutions) to shift servicing to low‑cost digital channels, defend NIM (now **1.57%**) via better cross‑sell, and support growth in mortgages and unsecured lending.
3. **Lending, Risk & Analytics for Mortgage and Unsecured Growth:** Deploy advanced analytics and AI (via **TCS AI.Cloud** and TCS risk analytics services) to optimise pricing and risk across the **£20.1bn** loan book, improve performance of new unsecured lending and credit card products (currently **£0.9bn** balances), and better align capital usage with the bank’s strong **CET1 23.9%** position.

## Slide 3: Strategic Priorities

1. **Prime Mortgages & Selective Corporate Lending Growth**  
   The bank is pursuing growth in its core lending franchise, with total customer loans rising to **£20.1bn** (from **£19.8bn**), including **prime residential mortgages of £16.5bn** (2023: **£16.4bn**), **Buy to Let mortgages of £2.3bn** (2023: **£2.0bn**) and **corporate net lending of £0.6bn** (2023: **£0.5bn**), although residential mortgage completions declined from **£3.9bn to £3.2bn**. No explicit dedicated budget is disclosed; this growth is being delivered within the bank’s ongoing operations and strategic spend.

2. **Unsecured Lending & New Credit Card Expansion**  
   The bank is expanding into higher‑yield unsecured lending, “including the launch of a new credit card product,” with unsecured balances totalling **£0.9bn** (flat vs **£0.9bn** in 2023), signalling an early‑stage or disciplined build‑out. Budget is not separately disclosed, but is likely part of overall **strategic expenditure of £33.6m in 2024** (vs **£32.5m** in 2023), with a multi‑year timeline for portfolio scale‑up.

3. **IT Simplification & Core Banking Platform Transformation**  
   Management reports “significant progress made in simplifying the Bank's IT infrastructure” and continued work on the “core banking platform migration,” indicating a large, multi‑year transformation targeting reduced complexity, better resilience and lower run‑costs. Technology and change spending is captured within **strategic expenditure of £33.6m in 2024** (up from **£32.5m**), and benefits are expected over several years as legacy platforms are retired.

4. **Digital Channels & Customer Experience Modernisation**  
   The bank is “investing in enhancing digital capabilities and improving customer experience, with further development of mobile and online banking platforms,” aiming to shift more interactions to digital, improve satisfaction and create cross‑sell opportunities. No stand‑alone budget is disclosed; spend is absorbed within **operating expenses of £291.0m** and overall strategic expenditure.

5. **ESG Strengthening & Ethical Bank Positioning**  
   As an ethical bank, Co‑operative is “strengthened ESG credentials, including ongoing commitments to sustainability and community initiatives,” seeking to reinforce its brand and align lending with sustainability outcomes. ESG‑related spend is not broken out in the RNS and is embedded in BAU operations and project budgets, with an ongoing, open‑ended timeline.

## Slide 9: Proposed Engagement Model

**Recommended Entry Point**  
Anchor the initial engagement on **IT Simplification & Core Banking Platform Transformation**, where the bank is already investing (**£33.6m** strategic expenditure in 2024) and has clear pain points linked to its **67%** underlying cost:income ratio.

**3‑Phase Roadmap**

1. **Phase 1 – Diagnostic & Value‑Case Definition (3–4 months)**  
   - Assess current core banking migration status, IT landscape and operating model.  
   - Quantify value levers: targeted reduction in run‑costs, legacy decommissioning opportunities, and dependency impacts on digital and lending initiatives.  
   - Define target architecture and migration blueprint leveraging **TCS BaNCS**, **TCS MasterCraft** and **ignio AIOps**; create a joint business case linked to cost:income improvements.

2. **Phase 2 – Pilot Migration & Digital/Lending Alignment (6–12 months)**  
   - Execute pilot migrations for selected products or customer segments using **TCS MasterCraft** for data and test automation.  
   - Implement **ignio AIOps** for observability and incident management across legacy and new environments.  
   - Launch 1–2 associated front‑to‑back journeys (e.g., a mortgage or unsecured lending journey) on the new platform to demonstrate end‑to‑end benefits.

3. **Phase 3 – Scale‑Up, Legacy Decommissioning & ESG Integration (24–36 months)**  
   - Scale core migration across product lines; progressively decommission legacy platforms and re‑negotiate infrastructure contracts.  
   - Industrialise digital channel enhancements and analytics (via **TCS AI.Cloud**) to support mortgage, unsecured and ESG‑aligned lending growth.  
   - Embed ESG data and reporting into the transformed architecture, aligning with the bank’s ethical positioning.

**Preferred Commercial Model and Rationale**  
Adopt a **hybrid commercial model**: time‑and‑materials for early diagnostic and design (Phase 1), transitioning to **milestone‑based fixed‑price** for pilot and scale migrations (Phases 2–3), with **select outcome‑linked incentives** tied to agreed metrics such as reduction in IT run‑costs, decommissioned applications, or improved operational KPIs. This balances flexibility and control for the bank while aligning TCS incentives with realised transformation value.
